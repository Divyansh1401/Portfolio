### Introduction
Splitwise is a popular **expense-sharing app** designed to simplify financial arrangements among friends, roommates, and groups.

Users input **expenses**, **categorise them**, and Splitwise calculates each person's share, making it easy to settle debts without hassle.

---

### Aim
Aim is to understand the **usability and cognitive ergonomics** of the app and find scope of improvements based on user study.

The primary research was based on studying and knowing the usage of the Splitwise application in daily life. ### User Definition
Splitwise is an expense-sharing app tailored for **tech-savvy users aged 16 to 30**, fluent in English, residing in **urban areas**, and **comfortable with technology**. This app simplifies **expense management** for roommates, friends, couples, travelers, students, family members, coworkers, and event organisers within this demographic.

### Heuristic Analysis
Heuristic analysis is a usability evaluation method used to assess the user-friendliness of a digital interface or system. It involves expert evaluators systematically examining a user interface against a set of predefined usability principles or "heuristics" to identify potential usability issues and areas for improvement. These heuristics are typically based on established usability guidelines and best practice.

*(Insights from Heuristic Analysis — see slide images)*

### Defining Tasks
We then defined **5 tasks** that covered all the key functions of the app and are essential to its purpose, then for each task we did a **Hierarchical Task Analysis (HTA)**.

The HTA was then followed by a **SHERPA analysis** to identify possible error points. We concluded with a **cognitive walkthrough**.

#### Hierarchical Task Analysis (HTA)
A systematic method used for analysing tasks. It provides a detailed, step-by-step breakdown of how individuals perform tasks, highlighting the knowledge, judgments, and actions involved.

#### SHERPA Analysis
A qualitative method used for assessing human reliability within a system, mainly focusing on operator abilities and behaviours. This method employs Hierarchical Task Analysis at a basic level for input. It involves specific steps for analysing human reliability.

#### Cognitive Walkthrough
An approach to usability testing and user interface design is the cognitive walkthrough. It involves a team of evaluators replicating typical users' mental processes as they interact with an app or interface to complete particular activities. The evaluators are typically designers or usability experts.

### Task 1 — Create a group of family members in Splitwise

#### HTA
- **T1**: Access Groups Section
  - Tap the "Groups" tab or option in the app's navigation menu
  - **T1.1**: Create a New Group
    - Tap the "Create Group" button or similar option
    - **T1.1.1**: Group Information
      - Enter the group name
      - Add type of group
    - **T1.1.2**: Add Members to the Group
      - Tap the "Add Members" or "Invite Friends" button
      - Search for members to add
      - Confirm selection
  - **T1.4**: Save/Review the group information and members
    - Tap the "Groups" tab or option in the app to confirm
    - Use "Save" button to finalise the group creation

#### Cognitive Walkthrough
1. **Open the App** — App icon is recognisable and launches swiftly.
2. **Navigate to groups** — Find the "Groups" section within the app, typically in the bottom navigation bar. It's easily identifiable by its label or icon.
3. **Create a New Group** — In the "Groups" section, look for an option like "Create New Group." It's typically a button or a plus icon. This step is intuitive and straightforward.
4. **Name the Group** — The text field is clear and awaits your input. You type in the name, for example, "Family."
5. **Add Members** — You tap on an "Add Members" button, which leads you to your contacts or allows you to enter email addresses. You select the people you want to include.
6. **Review and Confirm** — You see the group name and the list of members you've added. If everything looks correct, you tap "Create" or "Confirm." The app provides clear feedback, acknowledging the new group's creation.
